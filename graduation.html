Graduation & CHSI Guidance

If you are approaching the completion of your studies or have recently graduated, this page provides guidance on handling graduation documents, verifying your academic records through CHSI and maintaining accurate information for future recognition.

Before Graduation

Before leaving China or finalising your university procedures, collect all final academic documents and verify that your personal data is consistent across your diploma, transcript, passport and university records. Retain digital and physical copies of your documents to safeguard against loss.

Register Your Graduation with the Office

Notify the Cultural Office upon completing your studies. Provide your diploma, transcript, any verification documents and updated contact information. Registering ensures that the Office can assist you with future inquiries or documentation requests.

What is CHSI?

The Chinese Higher Education Student Information and Career Center (CHSI) is a platform that provides verification of higher education records in China. Ensuring that your academic data is accurate and consistent in CHSI is essential for future academic or professional recognition.

Common CHSI Problems

Common issues include discrepancies in names, passport information or academic details between your diploma and CHSI records. Resolving these discrepancies promptly helps avoid delays in recognition or further processing.

What Graduates Should Keep

Retain copies of your diploma, transcript, CHSI verification, passport, visa records and any other supporting documents. These documents may be required for future study, employment or official purposes.

If You Discover a Problem Later

If you encounter a data or verification issue after graduation, gather all relevant documents and contact the appropriate entity—whether your university, CHSI or the Cultural Office—as soon as possible. Early resolution is always easier than late correction.
