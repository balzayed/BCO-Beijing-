Guidance for New Students

Preparing to study in a new country can feel overwhelming. This page provides essential guidance to help you transition smoothly into your academic life in China.

Before You Apply

Before applying to a university in China, carefully review your program of interest, language of instruction, admission requirements and application deadlines. Ensure that your academic documents are properly prepared, translated where necessary and submitted according to the university’s instructions.

Scholarships

Explore scholarship opportunities offered by Chinese government programmes, individual universities and provincial authorities. Each scholarship has its own eligibility requirements and application deadlines—be sure to check official sources for the latest information.

Before Arrival

After receiving your admission offer, you must obtain the appropriate visa and prepare necessary documents for travel. Keep copies of your admission letter, visa, passport, and important contact information accessible. Arrange your accommodation or confirm your dormitory details before you leave.

Upon Arrival

Once you arrive in China, prioritise getting to your accommodation safely, securing local connectivity (SIM card or Wi‑Fi), completing any required health checks, and finalising your university registration. Make sure to collect your student ID and review your course schedule.

Accommodation

You may choose to live in university dormitories, serviced apartments or private accommodation. Off‑campus housing requires compliance with local regulations, including registering your address with the local authorities. Weigh your budget, distance to campus and personal preferences when selecting a place to live.

Important Documents

Keep secure copies of your passport, visa/residence permit, admission letter, insurance information, academic records and emergency contacts. Store digital copies online in case of loss. These documents are needed for university registration, visa renewals and other official procedures.

Register with the Cultural Office

All Bahraini students studying in China should register with the Bahrain Cultural Office in Beijing as soon as possible after arrival. Registration ensures that your information is on file and that you have access to support from the Office during your studies.
