Frequently Asked Questions

This section addresses common questions about registration, services, documents, academic requests and graduation procedures.

Do I need to register with the Cultural Office?

Yes. Bahraini students studying in China should register with the Cultural Office so that their student file can be created and maintained properly. Registration allows the Office to communicate with you and provide support when needed.

What documents do I need to register?

You will need a copy of your passport, admission or enrolment letter, visa or residence permit details, contact information, address in China, academic programme details and emergency contact information. Additional documents may be requested depending on your situation.

When should I update my record?

Please update your record whenever there is a material change in your address, contact details, university, programme or study status. Accurate records help the Office support you effectively.

Can I request an official letter?

Yes. Where your request falls within the Office’s role and the necessary information is provided, we can issue official letters, confirmations or certificates. You will need to provide the purpose of the letter, recipient details and any required supporting documents.

Can I change my major?

You may request a change of major by submitting a formal request with supporting documents. The Cultural Office will review the request in light of academic and procedural considerations. Approval is not automatic and may involve coordination with your university.

What should I do before graduation?

Before graduation, collect your diploma and transcript, verify your personal data for consistency, and ensure that your CHSI record reflects accurate information. Keep secure copies of all final documents.

What is CHSI?

CHSI is the Chinese Higher Education Student Information and Career Center, which verifies higher education records in China. Accurate CHSI records are essential for future recognition of your qualifications.

When should I contact the Embassy instead of the Cultural Office?

The Embassy handles consular and diplomatic matters and should be contacted in cases of serious emergencies, such as loss of passport, legal issues or situations requiring embassy intervention. The Cultural Office supports academic and administrative matters related to your studies.
